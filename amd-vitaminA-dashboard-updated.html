Why Elderly Are More Vulnerable
Older adults absorb fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) less efficiently due to reduced gut function. Fixed incomes restrict dietary diversity. Social isolation leads to poor eating patterns. Polypharmacy (multiple medicines) can deplete vitamin A stores.
VAD + AMD: The Double Burden
Vitamin A deficiency worsens AMD by impairing the visual cycle (retinal regeneration). Low retinol levels reduce RPE cell repair capacity, accelerating photoreceptor degeneration. Correcting VAD in elderly AMD patients slows disease progression by up to 28% (AIIMS data).
Screening Gaps in India
Only 12% of elderly Indians have undergone a dilated eye exam. Average AMD diagnosis happens 3.2 years after symptom onset. Rural elderly have almost no access to OCT scanning. Vitamin A levels are almost never tested in standard geriatric evaluations.

Age-related Macular Degeneration ‚Äî Indian Epidemiology

AMD is a progressive degeneration of the macula causing central vision loss. In India, urban prevalence is higher (8.3%) vs rural (5.9%), attributed to diet, UV exposure, and delayed diagnosis. South and North India show highest burden. The condition predominantly affects 60+ age group, with women at slightly higher risk (OR 1.3).

Prevention Strategies & National Programs

India has robust national programs addressing both AMD (under NPCB) and VAD (under POSHAN Abhiyaan, NHM). Primary prevention focuses on diet, lifestyle modification, and early screening. Secondary prevention relies on regular fundus examinations and Vitamin A supplementation rounds.

AMD Prevention ‚Äî Lifestyle & Dietary
Smoking Cessation
Reduces AMD risk by 3.6√ó. India: 28.6% male smokers. NRT patches/gums + counseling under NTCP. Strongest modifiable risk factor.
Dietary Antioxidants
Increase lutein/zeaxanthin-rich foods: spinach, methi, eggs, yellow maize, citrus. Target 6mg lutein + 2mg zeaxanthin daily from diet.
UV Protection
UV400 sunglasses mandatory outdoors in India's tropical climate. Reduces chronic photodamage. Wide-brim hats recommended for outdoor workers.
Blood Pressure & Sugar Control
Maintain BP <130/80 mmHg, HbA1c <7%. HTN and DM both increase AMD progression risk significantly in Indian patients.
Regular Eye Screening
Annual fundus examination after age 50. OCT scan recommended every 6 months for intermediate AMD. Amsler grid self-monitoring weekly.

Zinc is one of the most highly concentrated minerals in the human retina ‚Äî particularly in the retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) and choroid.
